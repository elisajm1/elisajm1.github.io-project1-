Facts about honey bees

Honey bees are highly important to the ecosystem. They're pollinators for flowers, fruits and vegetables. Honey bees live in hives and the hives' members are divided into three types:

QUEEN: The whole hive is run by one queen whose job is to lay eggs.

WORKERS: All of the workers are female and their roles are to forage for food, build and protect the hive, clean and circulate air.

DRONES: Drones on the other hand are male bees. Their purpose is to mate with the new queen. During the spring and summer several hundred drones live in each hive, but ones winter comes and the hive goes into survival mode, the drones get kicked out.
